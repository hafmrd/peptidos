## Page 4 [content]
- **Title**: SEMAGLUTIDE - Overview
- **Content**: Semaglutide is a glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist, a 31-amino acid analogue of human GLP-1 with 94% homology. Approved by FDA for type 2 diabetes (Ozempic) and obesity (Wegovy). Mechanism: activates GLP-1 receptors to stimulate insulin secretion, suppress glucagon, delay gastric emptying, and reduce appetite via central pathways. Clinical trials (STEP program) show 15-20% body weight reduction at 68 weeks with 2.4mg dose.

## Page 7 [content]
- **Title**: TIRZEPATIDE - Overview
- **Content**: Tirzepatide is a novel dual glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) and glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist. A 39-amino acid peptide with a C20 fatty diacid moiety for albumin binding and extended half-life. Approved as Mounjaro (diabetes) and Zepbound (obesity). SURPASS clinical program demonstrated superior HbA1c reduction vs. all comparators. SURMOUNT trials showed 15-22.5% weight loss depending on dose.

## Page 10 [content]
- **Title**: RETATRUTIDE - Overview
- **Content**: Retatrutide (LY3437943) is a first-in-class triple hormone receptor agonist targeting GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon receptors simultaneously. A 39-amino acid peptide conjugated to C20 fatty diacid. Phase 2 trials demonstrated unprecedented 24.2% mean weight reduction at 48 weeks with 12mg dose (NEJM 2023). Triple agonism may increase energy expenditure beyond GLP-1/GIP dual agonists. 100% of participants achieved >5% weight loss at 8mg+ doses.

## Page 16 [content]
- **Title**: GHK-CU - Overview
- **Content**: GHK-Cu is a tripeptide (glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine) complexed with copper(II), first isolated from human plasma. A key regulator of tissue remodeling with over 40 years of research. Functions: upregulates ~4000 human genes involved in regenerative processes, stimulates collagen and elastin synthesis, promotes wound healing, and exhibits potent antioxidant activity. GHK levels decline from ~200 ng/mL at age 20 to ~80 ng/mL at age 60. Copper ion (Cu2+) is essential for its biological activity.

## Page 19 [content]
- **Title**: CJC-1295 w/o DAC - Overview
- **Content**: CJC-1295 without DAC (also known as Mod GRF 1-29) is a 29-amino acid analogue of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH). The "w/o DAC" version provides a short, natural pulsatile GH release mimicking endogenous physiology. Unlike CJC-1295 with DAC, it does not cause GH bleed (continuous elevation) but rather discrete pulses. Best used in combination with a GHRP (like Ipamorelin) for synergistic effect. Research shows increased GH and IGF-1 levels without prolactin or cortisol elevation.

- **Title**: HGH (Somatropin) - Overview
- **Content**: Human Growth Hormone (Somatropin) is a 191-amino acid polypeptide produced by the anterior pituitary gland. Essential for growth, cell regeneration, and metabolism. Recombinant HGH (rHGH) is identical to natural pituitary GH. Approved indications include GH deficiency, Turner syndrome, chronic kidney disease, and Prader-Willi syndrome. Off-label research areas: anti-aging, body composition, recovery enhancement, and metabolic optimization.

- **Content**: TB-500 is the synthetic version of Thymosin Beta-4 (TB4), a 43-amino acid regenerative peptide present in virtually all human and animal cells. - **Content**: Semax is a synthetic heptapeptide (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) derived from the adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) fragment 4-10. ## Page 31 [content]
- **Title**: IPAMORELIN - Overview
- **Content**: Ipamorelin is a pentapeptide growth hormone secretagogue that selectively stimulates the pituitary gland to release growth hormone through activation of ghrelin receptors (GHS-R). Unlike other GHRPs (GHRP-2, GHRP-6), Ipamorelin is highly selective and does not significantly increase cortisol, prolactin, or aldosterone. This makes it the "cleanest" GHRP available for research. It mimics the natural GH pulsatile release pattern. Ideal for research on body composition, recovery, and anti-aging protocols.

## Page 34 [content]
- **Title**: EPITHALON - Overview
- **Content**: Epithalon (Epithalone, Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) is a tetrapeptide with potent telomerase-activating properties. Developed by Russian scientist Professor Vladimir Khavinson. Mechanism: activates telomerase enzyme, which elongates telomeres (protective caps at chromosome ends). Telomere shortening is a key hallmark of cellular aging. Clinical studies (20+ years in Russia) show increased telomere length, normalized melatonin secretion, improved immune function, and potential lifespan extension. One of the most researched geroprotective peptides globally.

## Page 37 [content]
- **Title**: NAD+ - Overview
- **Content**: NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide) is an essential coenzyme found in all living cells, critical for energy metabolism (ATP production via oxidative phosphorylation), DNA repair (as substrate for PARP enzymes), and cellular signaling. NAD+ levels decline by approximately 50% between age 20 and 50. NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR) and direct NAD+ administration are actively researched for aging intervention, metabolic health, neuroprotection, and exercise performance. Key role in sirtuin activation (SIRT1-SIRT7), the "longevity genes."
